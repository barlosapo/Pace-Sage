# VDOT Pace Zone Reference

This document provides a quick-reference table for VDOT values 40–65,
covering the range typical for recreational to competitive marathoners.

## How to use this table

1. Find your VDOT from a recent race using `pace-sage zones --recent-race <dist>:<time>`
2. Read across the row to find your training paces
3. Use paces as prescribed in the training plan phases

## Zone Descriptions

### Easy (76–79% VO₂max)
The majority of your weekly mileage. Conversational pace. Builds aerobic base,
promotes recovery between hard sessions, and strengthens connective tissue.
Run these on feel — slow down if needed.

### Marathon Pace (84% VO₂max)
Goal race pace. Used in the latter miles of long runs during Build and Peak phases.
Running at MP teaches your body the specific physiological demands of race day.

### Threshold (88% VO₂max)
"Comfortably hard" — you could speak in short sentences. Improves your lactate
threshold, the pace you can sustain for roughly 60 minutes. Prescribed as
continuous tempos (20–40 min) or cruise intervals (2–3 mi repeats).

### Interval (98% VO₂max)
Hard effort targeting VO₂max. Typically run as 800m–1600m repeats on a track
with equal or slightly shorter rest. Each rep should feel like you're working
hard but in control.

### Repetition (105% VO₂max)
Short, fast efforts (200m–400m) focused on running economy and speed. Full
recovery between reps. Used sparingly — mostly early in the plan or in tune-up
races.

## Estimating Your VDOT

The most reliable VDOT estimates come from:
1. A recent race (within 6 weeks) at full effort
2. A time trial over 5K–half marathon distance
3. A well-paced long tempo run

Avoid estimating from easy runs — they're intentionally run below potential.
